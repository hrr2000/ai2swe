## Motivation

A **70B** parameter model at BF16 needs **140 GB** just for weights — multi-GPU or impossible on edge. **INT8** halves; **INT4** quarters (roughly) with clever packing.

## Post-training quantization (PTQ)

Calibrate scale/zero-point on a small **representative batch**, then round weights. Fast, no retrain, but can **hurt perplexity** if done naively on LLMs.

## Quantization-aware training (QAT)

Simulate low-bit ops during training so weights adapt — best accuracy, highest cost.

## LLM-specific PTQ

- **GPTQ** — layer-wise Hessian-informed rounding for 4-bit weights.
- **AWQ** — protects **salient** weight channels based on activation stats.
- **GGUF / llama.cpp** stacks — CPU-friendly block quant formats.

## Key takeaways

- Quantization trades **numeric precision** for **memory + throughput**.
- **PTQ** is default for shipping; **QAT** when metrics regress.
- Pick format (**GGUF, ONNX, TensorRT-LLM**) based on your **serving runtime**, not vibes.
