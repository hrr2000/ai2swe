## Pattern

Append instructions like:

> “Think step by step. Put reasoning inside `<think>` tags and the final answer after `####`.”

Models trained with **RL on math** or **synthetic CoT** data respond better than tiny instruct models.

  code="printf debugging before returning from a recursive function"
  ai="CoT — externalized scratchpad tokens the model conditions its own answer on"
/>

## When it helps

- Arithmetic, logic puzzles, **multi-constraint scheduling**.
- **Code review** where intermediate hypotheses catch bugs.

## When it hurts

- **Latency + tokens** double if you keep reasoning in the user-visible stream.
- **Overconfidence** — fluent reasoning can justify wrong conclusions.

<Callout type="warning">
Do not expose raw CoT in **regulated** domains without human review — treat it as **draft internal notes**, not customer-facing truth.
</Callout>

## Variants

- **Zero-shot CoT** — literally prefix “Let’s think step by step.” (Surprisingly effective on some benchmarks.)
- **Tree-of-thought / self-consistency** — sample multiple chains, majority vote — costs scale linearly with samples.

---

## Key takeaways

- CoT trades **tokens** for **reliability** on hard reasoning tasks.
- Structure outputs (**tags**, **JSON**) so you can **strip** reasoning from UX while logging it for debug.
- Measure on **your** eval suite — not every model benefits equally.
