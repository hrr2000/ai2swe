## L2 regularization (“weight decay”)

Add a penalty proportional to `||θ||²` to the loss:

```text
L' = L + (λ/2) ||θ||²
```

Updates shrink weights toward zero unless data strongly needs them. In **AdamW**, decoupled **weight decay** applies directly to weights each step — the modern default in Transformer fine-tuning.

## Dropout

During training, randomly **zero** hidden units with probability `p`. Forces redundant representations — no single neuron can carry the whole job. At inference, use **eval mode** so dropout is disabled and scales are compensated (or use inverted dropout).

## Early stopping

Stop training when **validation loss** stops improving for `k` checks. Cheap, effective, and prevents the “train forever until you memorize” failure mode.

## Label smoothing

For classification, soften one-hot targets slightly so the model is not infinitely confident on noisy labels — reduces overfitting to annotation mistakes.

<Callout type="tip">
Regularization strengths (`λ`, `p`, smoothing) are **hyperparameters**. Log-scale sweeps; never copy a blog’s magic numbers without measuring on your **own** val split.
</Callout>

---

## Key takeaways

- **L2 / weight decay** shrinks unused weights.
- **Dropout** adds noise during training; disable in eval.
- **Early stopping** is free insurance when you lack infinite GPUs.
