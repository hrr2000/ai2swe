## Response-based distillation

Teacher produces **softmax probabilities** `p_T` over vocabulary; student minimizes **KL(p_T || p_S)** on the same inputs. Dark knowledge in **low-probability** classes carries regularization signal.

## Feature distillation

Match **intermediate representations** (hidden states, attention patterns) — harder to wire but can preserve reasoning traces.

## When it ships value

- Need **sub-100ms** latency on CPU/edge.
- Teacher is **API-bound** (privacy) — distill once, deploy student on-prem.

## Risks

- Student inherits **teacher biases**.
- **Distribution shift** — if student inputs differ from teacher training mix, mimicry breaks.

<Callout type="tip">
Pair distillation with **task-specific evals** — perplexity matching ≠ business metric matching.
</Callout>

---

## Key takeaways

- Distillation compresses **behavior**, not just weights.
- KL on **soft labels** is the classic objective; feature matching is optional spice.
- Validate **safety** independently — smaller ≠ safer.
