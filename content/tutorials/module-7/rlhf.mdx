## Three stages (classic recipe)

1. **SFT** — imitation learning on demonstrations (assistant behavior baseline).
2. **Reward modeling (RM)** — humans pick better answer A vs B; train `r(x, y)` to predict preference.
3. **RL fine-tune** — maximize `E[r]` subject to **KL divergence** vs SFT policy so answers stay coherent.

  code="Train a linter with human code review labels, then CI fails builds that violate learned style"
  ai="RM = learned preference linter; RL pushes logits toward passing it"

## Why KL control

Pure RL on a learned reward **overfits** — the model hacks the reward with gibberish high scores. KL to the reference LM keeps **token distribution** recognizable.

## Modern alternatives

- **DPO / IPO** — optimize preferences **without** explicit RL loops; simpler stability in small teams.
- **RLAIF** — replace human labels with stronger model judgments (still debatable quality).

<Callout type="warning">
RLHF infrastructure is **not** a weekend project — budget MLOps, label ops, and red-team loops comparable to shipping a payments service.
</Callout>

---

## Key takeaways

- RLHF aligns models to **human taste**, not just likelihood.
- **Reward hacking** is real — KL penalties and diverse evals mitigate.
- Consider **DPO-class** methods before building PPO clusters in-house.
