## The core problem

Language is **non-local**. In *“The animal didn’t cross the street because **it** was too tired,”* resolving **it** requires long-range context. Convolutions with small kernels or RNNs with finite hidden state struggle when dependencies skip far.

**Self-attention** lets **every token attend to every token** in one layer (subject to masking in decoders — more in advanced lessons).

## Q, K, V — three projections per token

For each position `i`, we form three vectors from the hidden state `x_i`:

- **Query `Q_i`** — “what am I looking for?”
- **Key `K_j`** — “what do I advertise as available at position j?”
- **Value `V_j`** — “if you pick me, here is the vector I contribute.”

Scores come from a **compatibility function**, usually scaled dot-product:

```text
s_ij = (Q_i · K_j) / √d_k
```

**Softmax** over `j` turns scores into weights `α_ij` that sum to 1. The output at `i` is the weighted sum `Σ_j α_ij V_j`.

## Why “self”?

Keys, queries, and values all come from the **same sequence**. Cross-attention (encoder–decoder stacks) instead lets one sequence attend into another — useful when decoding while reading a source sentence.

## Multi-head attention

One attention head might track **syntax** (subject–verb), another **coreference**, another **long-range theme**. **Multi-head** means multiple independent `(W_Q, W_K, W_V)` projections run in parallel; outputs are concatenated and mixed by another linear map.

## Complexity and masking

Dense self-attention costs **O(T²)** in sequence length for the attention matrix unless you adopt a sparse or linear variant. **Causal masking** in GPT-style decoders zeros out attention to future tokens so position `i` cannot peek ahead when generating left-to-right.

<Callout type="tip">
When you read `softmax(QKᵀ/√d)V`, mentally say: **normalize similarities into mixing weights, then take a convex combination of value vectors.** Everything else is engineering to make that stable at scale.
</Callout>

## Key takeaways

- Self-attention = **content-based routing** between positions using learned Q/K/V projections.
- **Softmax** makes the routing **differentiable** and sparse-ish (sharp or flat depending on logits).
- **Multi-head** + **residuals** + **FFN sublayers** = the standard Transformer block recipe.
