## One objective, many skills

A **causal language model** is trained (very roughly) to minimize **negative log-likelihood** of the next token given all previous tokens across a huge corpus:

```text
L = − Σ_t log p(x_t | x_<t ; θ)
```

That single objective — **predict the next token** — forces the network to internalize **grammar**, **facts** (as surface statistics), **API shapes**, and **reasoning patterns** that appear in training text.

<AnalogyBox
  code="Tab completion in your IDE — but trained on the public text of the internet"
  ai="Same mechanics: probability over a categorical vocabulary at each step"
/>

## How generation works at runtime

1. Tokenize the prompt → `x_1…x_T`.
2. Run a forward pass → logits over the vocabulary for position `T` (or each masked position during teacher forcing in training).
3. Convert logits to a distribution (`softmax`).
4. Pick the next token (argmax **greedy**, or **sample** with temperature / top-p).
5. **Append** the token, feed the longer prefix back in, repeat until `EOS` or max length.

Streaming UIs show tokens as they arrive because the server literally emits them **one at a time** from that loop.

## “Emergent” capabilities

Chain-of-thought style behavior, coding, translation — these can arise without explicit supervised heads because **next-token pressure** on diverse data implicitly rewards internal computations that reduce loss. That does **not** mean the model has beliefs or desires; it is still **conditional distribution estimation**.

<Callout type="caution">
Because the objective is **statistical continuation**, models can **confidently hallucinate** plausible-sounding tokens unsupported by your prompt or the real world. Retrieval (RAG), tools, and verification loops are engineering countermeasures — not optional polish.
</Callout>

## Temperature and top-p (preview)

**Temperature** scales logits before softmax: `T<1` sharpens the distribution (more deterministic), `T>1` flattens it (more random). **Nucleus (top-p)** sampling truncates the tail of unlikely tokens. Both control the **exploration–exploitation** tradeoff during decoding — covered in depth in a dedicated lesson when it ships.

## Key takeaways

- LLM generation = **repeated next-token prediction** with the growing prefix as context.
- Training aligns the model with **human-written continuation statistics**.
- Reliability issues (hallucination, bias, outdated facts) trace back to **objective and data**, not malice.
