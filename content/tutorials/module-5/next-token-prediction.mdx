# AI Is Just Autocomplete

When you use ChatGPT or Claude, it feels like you're talking to a conscious entity. But under the hood, the model is only doing one thing: **Predicting the Next Token.**

It is a statistical engine that has read almost everything ever written by humans and learned the patterns of our language.

## The Statistical Guess

If you give an LLM the prompt: *"To be, or not to..."*
The internal math looks something like this:
- **be**: 99.8% probability.
- **eat**: 0.01% probability.
- **code**: 0.001% probability.

The model picks the most likely next word and then... **it starts over**. 

It takes the original prompt *plus* the new word it just picked and runs the whole thing through the network again to pick the *next* next word.

## Generating 1 Token at a Time

This is why AI responses "stream" in character-by-character. The model is literally generating its own input for the next cycle.

1. **Cycle 1**: *"To be, or not to"* ➔ **"be"**
2. **Cycle 2**: *"To be, or not to be"* ➔ **","**
3. **Cycle 3**: *"To be, or not to be,"* ➔ **"that"**
...and so on.

## Emergent Intelligence

The "Magic" of AI is that simply by getting really, really good at predicting the next word, the model accidentally learned how to **Reason**.

To predict the next word in a complex Python function, the model has to implicitly understand logic, variable scope, and syntax. To predict the next word in a joke, it has to understand irony and timing.

Intelligence, it turns out, is a side-product of perfect pattern matching.

> [!CAUTION]  
> Because LLMs are just probability engines, they don't actually know "facts." They only know what word *should* come next based on their training data. This is the root cause of **Hallucinations**.
