## What people mean

Plot **model size** on the x-axis and **task accuracy** on the y-axis. Some benchmarks stay flat until a **threshold**, then jump — colloquially called **emergence**.

Examples folks cite: **few-shot ICL**, **instruction following**, **multi-hop reasoning** under CoT prompting.

  code="Latency cliff when you cross a GC generation boundary — smooth code until it isn’t"

## Skepticism is healthy

Recent work argues some “emergence” is **metric choice** (nonlinear transforms of smooth underlying loss) or **sparse discrete scores**. Regardless, **product teams** observe real cliffs when upgrading model tiers — treat them as empirical facts even if theory debates continue.

## Engineering takeaway

Do not assume **small model + clever prompt** replicates **large model behavior** on hard tasks. Maintain **tiered eval suites** so you know which features require which backbone.

<Callout type="insight">
Emergence thinking justifies **budget for bigger models** on the hardest 5% of tickets — while RAG + small models handle the long tail cheaply.
</Callout>

## Key takeaways

- **Nonlinear capability jumps** happen across scales — plan evals, not vibes.
- Debates continue on **definition** of emergence — shipping decisions shouldn’t wait on philosophy.
- Mix **tiers + tools + retrieval** instead of betting everything on one frontier model.
