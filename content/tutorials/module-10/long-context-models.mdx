## Why it matters

You can **paste** entire repos, PDFs, or multi-hour transcripts — reducing round trips through retrieval for some workflows.

## How it is possible

- **IO-aware attention** implementations (ring attention, sequence parallelism).
- **Better position codes** (YaRN, NTK-aware RoPE scaling).
- **Sparse / sliding** local-global hybrids in some architectures.

## Reality check

- **Needle-in-haystack** evals still show **recall cliffs** deep in context.
- **Cost** scales super-linearly with length for dense attention — read pricing tables.

<Callout type="insight">
Use **RAG + medium context** for factual grounding, **long context** for cross-file reasoning once snippets are already local — hybrid beats either extreme alone.
</Callout>

## Key takeaways

- Long context unlocks **new UX** (whole-repo questions) but not omniscience.
- **Cost + recall** still push teams toward retrieval.
- Benchmark on **your** documents — marketing numbers are upper bounds.
